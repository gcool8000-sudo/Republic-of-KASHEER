Overview

Kasheer is a Parliamentary Republic. The government is divided into three branches: Executive, Legislative, and Judicial. This separation prevents any one part from having too much power.

Legislative Branch — National Assembly of Kasheer (Bicameral)

Upper House

  • Reviews and improves proposed laws
  • Represents long-term national interests
  • Works with the Lower House to pass legislation

Lower House

  • Introduces most laws and budget plans
  • Represents citizens directly
  • Holds the Executive accountable through debates and questions

Term length: National Assembly members serve 5-year terms.

Executive Branch

Prime Minister (Head of Government)

  • Leads the Cabinet and runs day-to-day government
  • Proposes national policy and budget
  • Accountable to the National Assembly

Selection: Chosen by the National Assembly.

Term: 5 years.

President (Head of State)

  • Represents national unity and the constitution
  • Performs ceremonial duties and formal state functions
  • Ensures lawful transitions of government

Selection: Elected by National Assembly members.

Role: Mostly ceremonial (parliamentary model).

Judicial Branch — Supreme Court

The Supreme Court protects the constitution and ensures laws are applied fairly.

  • Judges are selected by an Independent Committee
  • Courts guarantee due process and the right to a fair trial
  • Judicial independence prevents political pressure
